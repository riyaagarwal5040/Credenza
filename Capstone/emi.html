What is EMI?

An EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment) is a fixed amount of money you pay every month to repay a loan. It includes Principal (the amount you borrowed) + Interest (the cost of borrowing).

Why EMIs Matter

  • Plan your monthly budget
  • Choose a loan that fits your income
  • Avoid financial stress
  • Compare lenders and interest rates
Simple Example: If you borrow ₹1,00,000 at 12% interest for 1 year, your EMI might be around ₹8,885 per month.

Types of EMI Structures

1. Fixed EMI

Same EMI amount throughout the loan.

Best for: Salaried individuals

2. Floating EMI

EMI amount changes based on market rates.

Best for: Home loans

3. Step-Up EMI

EMI starts low and increases every year.

Common for: Education loans

4. Step-Down EMI

EMI starts high and decreases each year.

Best for: Loans near retirement

5. Bullet Repayment

Pay only interest monthly. Principal at the end.

Used for: Gold/Business loans

How It Is Calculated

EMI = [P x R x (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N-1]

P = Loan Amount | R = Monthly Interest | N = Months
